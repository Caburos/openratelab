SECTION_01 // THE SITUATION

An email list with no
infrastructure behind it.

The brand had a growing subscriber list and was using it — occasionally. A newsletter went out when there was something obvious to say. A promotion when inventory needed moving. No flows were active. No automated sequences ran in the background. The list existed as a broadcast tool, not as a retention channel.

When you have no flows, every dollar email generates requires a person to write and schedule a campaign. The list's earning potential is capped by how much manual effort can go into it each month. And when the campaigns are sporadic, the list cools: open rates drop, deliverability erodes, and the subscribers who signed up engaged stop engaging over time.

The situation was not unusual. Most DTC brands at the growth stage are here: a real list, occasional campaigns, no backend automation. The gap between what the list is generating and what it could generate is the size of the infrastructure gap. We rebuild that infrastructure. Then we run it.

SECTION_02 // WHAT WAS BUILT
01 / FLOWS

COMPLETE FLOW ARCHITECTURE

Five core flows built from scratch: Welcome Series, Abandoned Cart, Checkout Abandonment, Post-Purchase, and Win Back. Each flow contains multiple emails with conditional branches so timing and messaging adapt to subscriber behaviour rather than firing on a fixed schedule. Together, these turn subscriber events into automated revenue that runs without manual input.

02 / COPY

BRAND-ACCURATE COPYWRITING

Every email in every flow is written to sound like the brand — not like a template with variables swapped out. The copywriting process starts with brand voice documentation and purchase data review before a single line is written. Flows and campaigns go through one revision cycle. The client reviews the final email before deployment. Copy, strategy, and scheduling are entirely handled.

03 / DELIVERABILITY

DNS AUTHENTICATION + LIST HYGIENE

Domain authentication — SPF, DKIM, DMARC — set up before the first send. Sending domain dedicated and warmed. Suppression lists maintained to prevent inactive subscribers from dragging down inbox placement. This work is invisible when it is done correctly. It becomes very visible when it is not.

04 / CAMPAIGNS

MONTHLY CAMPAIGN CALENDAR

A structured monthly send calendar replaces reactive sending. Campaigns are planned by purpose — product launches, seasonal windows, re-engagement sends — rather than triggered by inventory pressure. Every campaign is written, designed, and scheduled by us. The client's involvement is a final review before the email goes out.

SECTION_04 // WHAT 30% MEANS

Email at 30% is a
revenue channel, not a tool.

Industry benchmark for email attribution in ecommerce is 20–30% of total revenue. Stores below that threshold are underperforming — either the flows are missing, the copy is not converting, or the list is poorly segmented. Stores consistently at or above it have built email as a reliable, predictable channel rather than an occasional broadcast.

The difference matters beyond the percentage itself. When 30% of your revenue comes from email, that revenue is not dependent on ad spend. It does not fluctuate with CPM prices or Meta algorithm changes. It compounds as the list grows, as the flows are refined, and as the campaign cadence produces more data to improve the next month's output.

For this brand, getting to 30%+ required building what was missing: flows that run automatically, campaigns that are planned rather than reactive, and a deliverability foundation that keeps the emails landing in inboxes. The system runs without the founder's ongoing involvement. That is the goal — not just higher attribution, but higher attribution that does not cost more time to maintain.
